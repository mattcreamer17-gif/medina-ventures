Medina Ventures invests at the intersection of AI infrastructure, quantum technologies, and next-generation cybersecurity. The public thesis is clear: back exceptional founders building the enabling infrastructure for the next wave, at Seed and Series A, with roughly half of committed capital reserved for follow-on.

The platform advantage is equally clear. Exclusive early access through eMerge Americas. A team of operators who built and scaled category-defining infrastructure companies. A Miami-anchored pipeline that reaches Latin America.

The gap that small GPs describe privately is not sourcing, and not conviction. It is the bandwidth to take a portfolio company from term sheet to second enterprise contract. Partner time is finite. Platform teams at Seed-stage funds are thin by design. The practical question becomes: when a portco needs an orchestration layer designed above their core tech, who does the fund refer them to, and does that referral produce a deliverable the portco can actually ship.

Eduba is built for that referral. The 60/30/10 frame says that most problems belong to traditional code or rule-based logic, and only about a tenth belong to genuine LLM work. Applied to a Medina portfolio company, the output is a short, testable read: which parts of the stack benefit from an AI orchestration layer, which parts should stay deterministic, and which parts should not be built at all. The deliverable is the architecture, and then, when warranted, the bespoke implementation on top of the portco's existing stack.

For work that sits below the orchestration layer (production ML pipelines, data infrastructure, full-stack dev-heavy builds), Eduba partners with NLP Logix, which has been in machine learning since 2011 and runs over 150 data scientists. That split is the structural shape we think matches Medina Ventures' preferred referral posture.

The frame we apply to a portco's stack

Most of what gets labeled "AI work" is not AI work. Naming the layer is the engagement.

60%

Traditional code

Deterministic logic, databases, and pipelines. Faster, cheaper, and with stronger guarantees than an LLM would give. Most "AI" spend lands here by mistake.

30%

Rule-based logic

Structured decisions that belong in rules engines and workflow orchestration. Legible, auditable, and the right home for compliance-heavy operations.

10%

Genuine AI problems

The slice where an LLM or model earns its keep. Placed above the stack, governed with intent, integrated cleanly with the other two layers.
